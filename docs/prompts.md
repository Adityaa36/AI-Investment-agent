
Phase 1: Dashboard Redesign & Number Formatting Requirements
Redesign Layout and Presentation:

Switching the interface to a light theme with white and light gray backgrounds, using muted blue accents instead of neon colors.
Replacing most borders with whitespace and card grouping.
Creating an Executive Summary, Financial Snapshot, and Investment Thesis section.
Moving secondary information into collapsible sections.
Making it look like an institutional finance research dashboard (inspired by Morningstar, PitchBook, and Stripe) rather than a generic AI look.
Standardize Financial Number Formatting:

Converting market capitalization values into standard readable suffix notation (e.g. 1477689606000 to $1.48T, 125000000000 to $125B, 785000000 to $785M).
Converting decimal ratios to percentages (e.g. 0.85 to 85%, 0.0395 to 3.95%).
Formatting P/E ratios to one decimal place with the x multiplier (e.g. 29.84 to 29.8x).
Formatting Debt-to-Equity ratios with two decimal places (e.g. 0.73).
Representing confidence scores as percentages instead of decimals (e.g. 0.82 to 82%).
